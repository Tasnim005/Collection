Sometimes Allah allows some situations to go worse and closes all the doors only to see what his slave will do now. Will he still be grateful to his Lord and endure with patience or will he turn back on his knees.

If Allah willed, he could have given an easy exit to Musa (as). He might have taught him some better route where he wouldn't get stuck, But Nay! Allah wanted to test his conviction, so he allowed the situation to go worse.

And when the slave shows that ultimate conviction and belief in Allah like Musa (as) showed when got caught by Pharaoh from behind and Ocean in front, he didn't lose hope in his Lord and said with full conviction, "No, My Lord is with me and He will guide me." It is that conviction which Allah wants to see and then Allah makes a way out even if it was to split the ocean, make the fire cool or split the moon into pieces, Allah will do it for his slave.

Allah will never fail you. You just need to show your conviction.

You can't fight Qadr.

If something is written for you it'll be yours, nothing can stop that. Live your life at peace. Don't fill your heart with worry and anxiety. Your Rab, the greatest of the greatest has written your fate.

Trust him.

There's a reason why you didn't stop making dua for it.

Your heart can't let go, and your brain can't think of anything else. Your tongue can't stop expressing the same dua again and again.

Allah allows you to make that specific dua. Do you really think He won't answer you? He will give you what you deserve in the most beautiful way. If He allows you to raise your hands and make dua or pray for something, then it is only because He wants you to have it.

Have sabr and watch how Allah will change your life.

Maybe you want something so badly that you start getting impatient for your dua to be answered.

But the amazing thing is the gap between your duas and the day they are answered are those sweet moments where it's just you and Allah and you're begging your heart out to Him, feeling content that He's going to heal you and make everything okay. That closeness to Allah, that's so valuable.

Alhamdullilah for the times He lets us experience them.

Allah isn't ignoring your duaa.

He is simply just delaying His response because He loves hearing your voice calling out to Him. He hears you make the same dua in sujood everyday. He sees your tears before they even fall.

I know sometimes it may feel like your dua may never be answered, but in those moments remember who you are asking. You are asking the same Allah who had guided you to make this dua in the first place. Perhaps Allah (SWT) wants to see how much you can rely on him. He loves you more than your own mother, how can you not trust his plans for you? He loves you so much and by Him delaying His response, He is testing how much trust you have in Him and His plan.

So please my brothers and sisters, have patience and do not give up on your duas. your voice which you think doesn't go beyond your ceiling, it goes beyond 7 skies.
